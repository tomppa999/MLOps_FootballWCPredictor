## Key decisions
- Importance is logged as a CSV MLflow artifact (not as individual metrics) because it's a full DataFrame per model.
- The "best" run is separate from the tuning parent run to keep artifacts clean and queryable via `tags.role = 'best'`.
- Heatmap script lives in `src/models/` (not a separate `scripts/` folder) to match the project's `src/` layout.
- Permutation importance uses RPS as the scoring metric (consistent with the QA evaluation metric).

### Interpretation

1. **GLMs dominated CV but XGBoost won holdout.** The three Poisson-family models (negbin, bayesian, bivariate) had the best CV NLL but XGBoost generalized better on the unseen 2022 WC holdout — a meaningful sign of better out-of-sample calibration.
2. **Deep learning underperformed.** LSTM (2.7922) and CNN (2.9805) were notably worse than the statistical and tree-based models despite extensive tuning (50 trials each), and were the most expensive to train (~27min and ~11min respectively). Limited national-team data volume is the likely cause.
3. **SARIMAX was the weakest.** Best NLL of 3.0097 — the time-series approach struggles with this match-level prediction task where temporal ordering per team is sparse.
4. **Ridge needs more regularization headroom.** Its best alpha hit 989 at the boundary of the [1e-3, 1e3] search range, suggesting the search space should be widened to 1e5. It still underperformed significantly (2.8498) so unlikely to change the ranking.
5. **Bayesian Poisson was insensitive to hyperparameters.** Nearly all 40 trials landed in the 2.7543–2.7545 range, indicating the posterior is well-identified regardless of prior sigma or MCMC settings.
6. **Total wall-clock time:** ~91 minutes from start of Phase 1 (15:34) to pipeline complete (17:05).
